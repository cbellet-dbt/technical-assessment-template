üìã Pre-Meeting Preparation

Meeting Setup & Context

Objective: Conduct a comprehensive discovery to understand the customer's current data platform, pain points, and alignment with dbt value proposition. This meeting should take 60-90 minutes with 2-3 stakeholders.
üí° Pro Tip: Schedule this as a working session with relevant stakeholders present. Audio recording with permission helps capture details you might miss while taking notes.

Required Attendees

üìä Data Lead/Manager

Understands team structure, priorities, and organizational constraints. Usually the sponsor.

üîß Technical Lead

Can speak to current architecture, tools, pain points. Usually an engineer or architect.

üíº Business Stakeholder

Represents business impact and priorities. Can discuss ROI and strategic goals.

Pre-Meeting Checklist

  • Schedule 60-90 minute meeting with 2-3 key stakeholders
  • Send calendar invite with agenda 3-5 days before
  • Request recording permission via email
  • Ask them to have system diagram or architecture doc available
  • Prepare laptop/tablet with this guide accessible
  • Have blank assessment template open for note-taking
  • Print or digital version of this guide ready
  • Have dbt.com resources bookmarked for reference

0-5 min

Welcome & Context Setting

5-20 min

Organization & Context

20-40 min

Current State & Pain Points

40-55 min

Discuss objectives and expected outcomes

55-80 min

Summarize findings and agree on next actions

Opening Script (30 seconds)

"Thanks for taking the time to meet with us. Our goal today is to understand your current data platform setup, the challenges you're facing, and your business objectives. This will help us assess how dbt and modern data stack practices could benefit your organization. I'll be taking notes and we'll create a comprehensive assessment report that we can use to develop a tailored implementation roadmap. Does that sound good?"

General Interview Tips

Active Listening: Take notes but maintain eye contact. Don't interrupt. Let them finish their thoughts before asking follow-ups.
Probe Deeper: When they mention a pain point, ask "Tell me more about that" or "Can you give me a specific example?" Specifics help you understand impact.
Don't Sell (Yet): Focus on understanding. You can mention dbt solutions in later conversations. Here you're gathering facts.
Be Quantitative: Where possible, ask for numbers. "How many hours per week?" "What's the team size?" "How often does this happen?" Numbers make the assessment stronger.
